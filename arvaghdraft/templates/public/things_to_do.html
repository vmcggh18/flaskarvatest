Things to do

Being in the drumlin belt, Arva is surrounded by unspoiled landscape dotted with lakes and historic towns and villages and the surrounding low rolling hills and lakes are well suited to land and water based activities. The area is a paradise for anglers and is very popular with fishermen. The lakes such as Hollybank, Gulladoo and Rockfield offer excellent angling all year round.
Self-catering is popular and a range of self catering cottages and holiday homes are available in the vicinity of Arvagh.
Arvagh is within easy reach of Killykeen Forest Park, Cavan Adventure Centre, Cavan Museum and the WW1 Trench Experience, the Knights & Conquest Centre in Granard and the Royal Canal Greenway, which can be accessed in nearby Longford.

Fishing:

The area is very popular with fishermen and lakes such as Hollybank, Gulladoo and Rockfield offer excellent angling all year round. An international fishing tournament takes place every September. Bait, boat hire and tackle can be hired in Arvagh Town.

Self Catering:

There are several self catering accommodations scattered in the town and surrounding area, in the form of holiday chalets, villas, log cabins, apartments, and cottages, facilitating a comfortable and affordable stay in this region.

Noteworthy items

The famous Arvagh Agricultural Show takes place annually every July.

The "3 Province Festival" also takes place in Arva over 10 days from the last weekend in July until the first weekend in August. The festival includes music, theatre, comedy, art, competitions and other events.
